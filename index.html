The Problem

Recruiting runs on hours nobody has.

The sourcing treadmill.

Great recruiters spend their best hours on Boolean strings, list building, and profile review. The work matters, but it is not where deals close.

The follow-up gap.

Candidates go cold in days. Clients expect updates before they ask. Every missed touch is a placement at risk.

The admin drag.

Scheduling, ATS hygiene, notes, and status reports eat the margins of every day. It all has to happen, and none of it should fall on your closers.

One agent. Your entire back office.

01

Candidate Sourcing and Research.

Builds longlists overnight, enriches profiles, maps target companies, and surfaces candidates that match the brief, ranked and ready for review each morning.

02

Outreach and Follow-Up.

03

Scheduling and Coordination.

Client Communications.

Market and Compensation Intelligence.

Pipeline and ATS Hygiene.

Keeps records current, logs activity, updates stages, and produces clean reporting, so your data finally reflects your desk.

A Day With The Recruiting Agent

While your team recruits, the agent runs the desk.

6:30 AM

Overnight sourcing is done. A ranked longlist for the VP Engineering search is waiting, with profile notes and suggested outreach angles.

9:15 AM

Client update drafts for all four active searches are prepared and queued for partner review before the 10 AM calls.

12:40 PM

A candidate replies to a sequence. The agent flags it, pulls their file, and drafts a response with three interview slots already checked against the client's calendar.

3:00 PM

Comp research for tomorrow's offer negotiation is compiled: three sources, a recommended range, and the talking points.

6:45 PM

The desk report lands: pipeline movement, stalled candidates, tomorrow's priorities. The ATS is current. Nothing slipped.

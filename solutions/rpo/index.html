What does an AI recruiting agent do for an RPO?

RPO is a margin and SLA business. You win on the ability to scale a delivery team fast, hold time-to-fill and time-to-slate commitments, and do it profitably across accounts that each run different tools and hiring bars. The cost pressure is real: every hour a recruiter spends on manual sourcing and scheduling is an hour billed against a fixed-fee or per-hire model. The Recruiting Agent, built by Apollo[Claw] AI Consulting, takes on that operational load so your delivery pods produce more slates per recruiter and your unit economics improve.

It is a genuine AI recruiting agent that acts across the funnel: it reads a client requisition, runs agentic sourcing, applies the client's screening bar, and advances qualified candidates while keeping that client's system of record current.

Elastic capacity for requisition spikes

RPO demand is lumpy. A client opens 60 requisitions for a facility launch and expects a slate in weeks. Traditionally that means scrambling for contract recruiters, onboarding them to the client's process, and hoping they ramp before the deadline. The Recruiting Agent absorbs the surge in sourcing and first-touch outreach immediately, so your delivery team scales output without a hiring ramp, then dials back when volume normalizes without a layoff cycle.

Multi-client delivery with strict separation

An RPO handles many clients, and each client's data must stay separate. The Recruiting Agent is deployed as one agent per firm inside your environment, with no pooled data across clients and every action logged. That separation and audit trail is exactly what a client's procurement and compliance teams ask about before they sign.

How it fits per-client stacks and SLAs

RPO providers rarely control the tech stack. The Recruiting Agent is configured to work inside whatever applicant tracking system each client mandates, so your team is not forced onto one platform.

  • Workday and Greenhouse: sources against client requisitions and keeps candidate stages and activity accurate in the client's system of record.
  • Bullhorn and Ceipal: supports high-volume and contingent-style delivery with current candidate records and notes.
  • Email and calendar: runs interview scheduling and candidate communication against SLA timelines.

The metrics RPO leaders defend

RPO leaders report time to fill, time to slate, submittal-to-interview and interview-to-hire ratios, fulfillment rate, and delivery margin per account. The Recruiting Agent supports all of them by lifting sourcing throughput and cutting the scheduling and admin drag that erodes margin. Pipeline reporting produces client-ready views by account and requisition, so QBRs are backed by live data instead of a hand-built deck.

  • One agent per firm, no data pooled across your clients.
  • Client data stays in your environment and is never used to train outside models.
  • Full deployment, 90 days of optimization, then month to month, with direct founder access.

Governance for client audits and MSA terms

RPO contracts carry data-protection clauses, audit rights, and OFCCP obligations passed down from clients. The Recruiting Agent keeps a human in the loop on selection and offers, logs every action, and isolates each client's data, which supports EEOC and OFCCP compliance because people make the selection calls.

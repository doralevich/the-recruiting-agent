What does an AI recruiting agent do for technical recruiting?

Engineering talent is scarce, skeptical, and rarely actively looking. A backend engineer with the right distributed-systems background gets a dozen identical InMails a week and ignores all of them. The recruiter who wins is the one who understands the stack, references the candidate's actual work, and does not waste their time. The problem is that this depth does not scale by hand across a full req load. The Recruiting Agent, built by Apollo[Claw] AI Consulting, does the deep sourcing and personalization groundwork so technical recruiters spend their hours on real conversations and closing.

It is a working AI recruiting agent that reasons about technical fit. It reads an engineering requisition, runs agentic sourcing that distinguishes a Go backend engineer from someone who listed Go once, and starts outreach and follow-up that references the specific role and the candidate's background.

Sourcing on skill signal, not keyword soup

Keyword sourcing floods a technical req with false positives. The Recruiting Agent maps candidates by stack, seniority, domain, and the shape of their actual experience, so a recruiter reviews people who fit a senior platform role rather than everyone who typed the word "Kubernetes." It records that reasoning in your applicant tracking system so the shortlist is defensible.

Outreach engineers actually answer

Personalization at volume is the hard part of technical recruiting. The agent drafts messages that reference the specific team, the technical problem, and the candidate's relevant work, sequences follow-ups, and stops the instant someone replies or opts out. Every message is logged and a human approves the approach, which keeps outreach on brand and keeps selection decisions with people, supporting EEOC compliance.

How it fits your engineering hiring stack

The Recruiting Agent is configured around the systems technical teams already run, and it respects your existing interview loop.

  • Greenhouse and Lever: sources against open engineering reqs, keeps candidate stages and scorecards current, and logs sourcing rationale.
  • Your take-home and interview loop: handles interview scheduling across engineer panels and coordinates technical rounds.
  • Email and calendar: runs personalized outreach and schedules debriefs around busy engineering calendars.

Technical recruiting leaders watch outreach response rate, sourced-to-screen conversion, pass-through at the technical round, time to fill for hard reqs, and offer acceptance. The Recruiting Agent lifts the top and middle: sharper targeting raises response rate, better-fit candidates convert further into the loop, and reliable scheduling keeps engineers engaged in the interview panel. Pipeline reporting shows conversion by req and stage so leaders can see where a technical funnel leaks.

For a retained search firm, The Recruiting Agent handles the research-heavy groundwork of a mandate: it maps target companies, builds and prioritizes an off-market candidate list, profiles executives with tenure and mandate history, drafts discreet outreach in the consultant's voice, and maintains compensation and market intelligence throughout the engagement. It is one dedicated AI recruiting agent per firm, running inside your environment, so partners keep the judgment work and lose the desk research.

What can an AI agent do for an executive search firm?

Retained search is a research business before it is a sourcing business. A single mandate can require mapping an entire sub-sector, profiling 150 executives, and narrowing to a slate of eight or ten before a partner ever makes a call. That mapping is slow, and it is the part that delays a shortlist. The Recruiting Agent, built by Apollo[Claw] AI Consulting, runs that groundwork continuously so a consultant opens the engagement with a prioritized long list instead of a blank sheet.

Unlike a keyword tool, this is an AI recruiting agent that reasons across a mandate: it reads the position specification, identifies target companies and adjacent talent pools, runs agentic sourcing to surface executives who fit, and organizes them by relevance, tenure, and likelihood of a move.

Discretion and voice matter more here

Executive outreach lives or dies on tone. A generic InMail to a sitting CFO ends the conversation. The Recruiting Agent drafts outreach and follow-up in the consultant's own voice, referencing the specific mandate and the executive's background, and it holds for partner approval before anything sends. Nothing goes out automatically, which fits the confidentiality a retained engagement demands.

Market intelligence your partners can put in front of a client

Clients pay for insight, not just names. The agent researches compensation benchmarks, competitor org structures, and movement in the sector, and it assembles that into material a partner can bring to a client update. It refreshes as the search runs, so a status call is backed by current market data instead of a snapshot from the kickoff.

How it fits a retained workflow and your stack

The Recruiting Agent is configured around the systems a search firm already uses. It maintains candidate and company records, contact history, and search progress in your applicant tracking system, and it prepares interview and client-update logistics through your email and calendar.

  • Search-focused ATS and CRM such as Bullhorn or your firm's system: keeps long lists, slates, and off-limits records organized and current.
  • Compensation and market research: compiles benchmarks and org intel into consultant-ready notes.
  • Email and calendar: coordinates candidate and client meetings around partner availability.

For a corporate talent acquisition team, The Recruiting Agent adds recruiter capacity by taking on the operational load per requisition: it runs agentic sourcing, screens and organizes inbound applicants, coordinates interview scheduling across hiring managers, prepares hiring-manager updates, and keeps requisition records current in your applicant tracking system. What does an AI recruiting agent do for an in-house TA team?

Corporate recruiters are measured on time to fill, quality of hire, offer acceptance, and hiring-manager satisfaction, and they carry a full requisition load while doing intake calls, debriefs, and stakeholder management. The parts that stretch a recruiter thin are the operational ones: sourcing when applicant flow is low, screening a flood of inbound applicants when it is high, and the scheduling tetris across busy hiring-manager calendars. The Recruiting Agent, built by Apollo[Claw] AI Consulting, handles that layer so recruiters stay on candidate experience and hiring-manager partnership.

It is a working AI recruiting agent, not a career-site chatbot. It reads a requisition, runs agentic sourcing against it, applies your resume screening criteria to inbound applicants, and moves qualified people forward inside your workflow, taking multi-step actions rather than answering a single prompt.

Screening inbound volume without dropping candidates

A single corporate req can draw hundreds of applicants. Recruiters cannot read every one the day it arrives, and good candidates go cold in the queue. The Recruiting Agent reviews inbound applications against the requisition's must-haves, surfaces the strongest matches for recruiter review, and keeps candidates informed so no one sits in silence. It applies the same criteria to every applicant, which brings consistency the manual queue rarely achieves under load. People make the advance-or-decline call, which keeps selection decisions human and supports EEOC compliance.

Scheduling across hiring-manager calendars

Interview coordination is one of the biggest silent drains on TA time. The Recruiting Agent handles interview scheduling across panels and hiring-manager calendars, sends invites and reminders, and reschedules when someone drops, so recruiters are not playing calendar broker between candidates and busy managers.

How it fits your talent stack

The Recruiting Agent is configured around the systems your TA function already runs, and it works within your requisition and approval flows rather than around them.

  • Greenhouse and Lever: sources against open jobs, updates candidate stages, and logs activity so scorecards and pipelines stay accurate.
  • Workday: keeps requisition and candidate records aligned with your system of record.
  • Email and calendar: runs candidate communication and multi-panel interview scheduling.

The metrics TA leaders report on

Heads of talent report time to fill, cost per hire, source of hire, pipeline conversion, and hiring-manager satisfaction. The Recruiting Agent moves several at once: faster sourcing shortens time to fill, consistent screening protects quality, and reliable scheduling improves both candidate experience and hiring-manager satisfaction. Pipeline reporting gives leaders a live view by requisition and department without a recruiter building a spreadsheet.

  • One company, one agent, with no data pooled across employers.
  • Your candidate data stays in your environment and is never used to train outside models.
  • Full deployment, 90 days of optimization, then month to month, with direct founder access.

Governance your legal and compliance teams will ask about

In-house teams answer to legal, DEI, and audit. The Recruiting Agent keeps a human in the loop on every selection and offer decision, logs every action for audit, and keeps your data inside your environment, which supports EEOC and OFCCP compliance because people, not automation, decide who advances.

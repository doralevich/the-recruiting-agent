The Recruiting Agent runs interview scheduling automation for recruiters: it checks interviewer availability on your connected calendars, proposes times to the candidate, confirms the booking, sends invites and reminders, and reschedules when plans change. It writes each interview and status to your applicant tracking system as it goes. The days lost to scheduling email between an interested candidate and a confirmed slot close, without a coordinator chasing calendars by hand.

Candidates cool fast. The gap between a candidate saying yes and sitting in an interview is where momentum leaks away, and most of that gap is scheduling: matching calendars, proposing times, waiting for a reply, re-proposing when a slot is taken. It is pure coordination, and it is exactly what an agent should carry. The Recruiting Agent, a dedicated AI recruiting agent from Apollo[Claw] AI Consulting, closes that gap around the clock so an interested candidate can be booked overnight.

How scheduling works step by step

  1. Reads availability. The agent checks the real-time calendars of the interviewers on the loop, respecting existing meetings, time zones, and booking rules.
  2. Offers times to the candidate. It proposes open slots to the candidate over email or your channels, in your firm's voice.
  3. Books and confirms. When the candidate picks a time, the agent holds the slot, sends calendar invites to everyone, and confirms the details.
  4. Reminds both sides. It sends reminders ahead of the interview to candidate and interviewers, which is where no-shows are prevented.
  5. Handles reschedules. If someone needs to move, the agent finds a common slot and rebooks without a recruiter running the loop again.
  6. Logs to the ATS. Every interview, confirmation, reminder, and change is written to Bullhorn, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, or Ceipal, so the record stays current.

Panels, time zones, and reschedules

Simple one-on-one scheduling is easy; real recruiting is not. A panel needs three calendars to align. A candidate on the coast is three hours off the hiring manager. A confirmed interview falls through the morning of. These are the cases that eat a coordinator's day, and they are where the agent earns its place: it aligns multiple calendars, respects time zones, and rebooks a canceled slot without starting over.

Scheduling in the pipeline, not beside it

Scheduling is a link, not an island. Candidates arrive from candidate outreach and follow-up the moment they say yes, so there is no lag between interest and an offered time. Once an interview is booked, the agent updates pipeline reporting and prepares client updates, so the recruiter and client always know who is interviewing and when. One agent carries the candidate from reply to confirmed interview to a reported milestone.

A tech team runs multi-stage technical loops; a corporate talent function runs structured panels. Scheduling is configured to each. See tech recruiting for how the agent fits multi-stage technical loops.

Recruiter control and governance

Automation should not mean a recruiter loses the thread. The Recruiting Agent keeps the recruiter informed of every booking and hands off any exception, an executive who wants a personal touch, a sensitive candidate, a client with special rules, rather than forcing it. It runs as one agent per firm inside your environment, your calendar and candidate data stays in your systems and is never used to train outside models, and every scheduling action is logged. The agent coordinates; people handle the moments that call for judgment.

Interview scheduling FAQs

What is interview scheduling automation? +
Interview scheduling automation is when an AI recruiting agent reads interviewer availability, offers times to the candidate, books and confirms the slot, sends reminders, and handles reschedules. The Recruiting Agent does this and logs each step to your ATS.
Does it handle panel interviews and multiple time zones? +
Yes. The agent aligns several interviewers' calendars, respects time zones and booking rules, and finds a slot that works for the full panel and the candidate.
What happens when someone needs to reschedule? +
The agent finds a common open time and rebooks without a recruiter restarting the loop, then updates the invites and the ATS record.
Which calendars and ATS does it work with? +
It connects to your calendar and email and writes interview records to systems including Bullhorn, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, and Ceipal, configured around your existing stack.
Can a recruiter step in? +
Yes. The agent keeps the recruiter informed and hands off any exception, such as an executive candidate or a client with special rules, rather than forcing it through automation.

Interview formats, calendar tools, and volume vary by firm, so pricing is scoped per firm on a discovery call. There is no published price.

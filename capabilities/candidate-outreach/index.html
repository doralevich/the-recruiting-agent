The Recruiting Agent runs automated candidate outreach and follow-up as AI recruiting sequences: it drafts personalized messages in your firm's voice, spaces them across email and your channels, and follows up on a cadence until a candidate responds or the sequence completes. A recruiter reviews and approves before anything sends, and each message, reply, and status change is written back to your applicant tracking system. The result is that no sourced candidate goes dark because someone forgot the third touch.

Reply rates are made on follow-up. The first message rarely lands; the fourth often does. But follow-up is exactly the work that slips when a recruiter is on calls, in submittals, and prepping interviews. Candidates fall out of sequences not because they said no but because no one wrote again. The Recruiting Agent, a dedicated AI recruiting agent from Apollo[Claw] AI Consulting, owns that cadence so every candidate gets the full sequence, on time.

How the outreach agent works

Outreach automation earns trust only when the messages sound like your firm and a person stays in control of what goes out. The agent is built around both.

  1. Drafts in your voice. The agent writes each message from the candidate's background, the role, and your firm's tone, so outreach reads as personal, not as a mass blast.
  2. Sequences the touches. It builds a multi-step cadence across email and your connected channels, with sensible spacing between the initial message and each follow-up.
  3. Waits for recruiter approval. Messages queue for a recruiter to approve, edit, or hold. Human in the loop on outreach is a governance rule, not an option.
  4. Follows up and detects replies. The agent sends approved touches on schedule, recognizes when a candidate responds, stops the sequence, and routes the reply to the recruiter.
  5. Logs everything to the ATS. Every send, open, reply, and status change is written to Bullhorn, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, or Ceipal, so the record is complete without manual note-taking.

Sequences that adjust, not just fire

A fixed drip sends the next message no matter what. The Recruiting Agent reads context first. If a candidate replies with interest, it stops the automated cadence and hands off to your recruiter. If a candidate is out of office or asks for later, the timing shifts. If a role closes, the sequence ends. Outreach that reacts to signals keeps your firm from writing the wrong message at the wrong moment.

Fed by sourcing, aimed at the interview

Outreach is one link in a chain the same agent runs end to end. Candidates come from candidate sourcing with context already attached, so the first message references real detail. When a candidate says yes, the agent moves them into interview scheduling without a manual handoff. One agent carries the candidate from found to booked, and your recruiters step in at the moments that need judgment.

Built for your desk

A high-volume staffing desk and a corporate talent team run outreach differently: different cadence, different tone, different compliance posture. The agent is configured to each. See corporate talent acquisition for how outreach maps to an in-house model.

Candidate outreach FAQs

What is automated candidate outreach? +
Automated candidate outreach is when an AI recruiting agent drafts, personalizes, sequences, and follows up on messages to candidates on a set cadence. The Recruiting Agent does this in your firm's voice and logs every touch to your ATS, with a recruiter approving each send.
Does the agent send messages on its own? +
No. Every message queues for a recruiter to approve, edit, or hold before it sends. Human in the loop on outreach is a governance rule for The Recruiting Agent.
What happens when a candidate replies? +
The agent detects the reply, stops the automated sequence so no further scheduled message goes out, routes the response to your recruiter, and updates the candidate's status in your ATS.
Will the outreach sound like our firm? +
Yes. The agent drafts each message from the candidate's background and your firm's tone, so outreach reads as personal rather than as a mass template.
How does outreach connect to sourcing and scheduling? +
The same agent sources the candidate, drafts outreach with that context, and moves interested candidates into interview scheduling, so there is no manual handoff between steps.
What does it cost? +
Outreach volume, channels, and cadence vary by firm, so pricing is scoped per firm on a discovery call. There is no published price.

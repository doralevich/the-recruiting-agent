GLOSSARY

Recruiting AI, defined.

Plain definitions for the terms behind AI recruiting: what an AI recruiting agent is, how agentic sourcing works, and how screening stays compliant. The Recruiting Agent maintains these definitions as a reference for recruiting and talent acquisition leaders.

These are the terms you will meet while evaluating recruiting automation. For the full picture, start with what is an AI recruiting agent.

AI recruiting agent

Software that performs recruiting work end to end, sourcing candidates, drafting and sequencing outreach, scheduling interviews, and updating the applicant tracking system, rather than only answering questions. It takes actions across systems toward a goal and reports back.

Agentic sourcing

Sourcing performed by an autonomous agent that searches, enriches profiles, maps target companies, ranks candidates against a brief, and assembles a longlist without a recruiter running each query. It runs continuously, including overnight.

Candidate outreach automation

The drafting and sequencing of personalized candidate messages and follow-ups. A recruiting agent writes in the firm's voice, spaces follow-ups, and flags warm replies, so no candidate goes cold in the pipeline.

AI resume screening

The ranking of applicants against a role's requirements by an AI system that shows its reasoning. Compliant screening keeps a human in the loop for selection decisions and logs every step for EEOC and OFCCP record-keeping.

Recruiting copilot vs recruiting agent

A recruiting copilot assists a recruiter inside a tool, suggesting text or answers while the person drives. A recruiting agent does the task itself across systems and reports back. The difference is who performs the work.

Applicant tracking system (ATS)

The system of record for candidates, jobs, and stages, such as Bullhorn, Greenhouse, Lever, or Workday. An AI recruiting agent works on top of the ATS, reading and writing records while the ATS remains the source of truth.

Recruiting automation

Any software that removes manual steps from recruiting. An AI recruiting agent is the agentic form of recruiting automation: it chains steps together and adapts, rather than firing fixed rules.

Human in the loop

A design where a person approves consequential actions. In recruiting, outreach, client communications, screening decisions, and offers move only with a recruiter's approval, which keeps selection decisions with people.

Talent intelligence

Research on talent supply, compensation ranges, and competitor moves that informs intake calls and offers. A recruiting agent compiles it from multiple sources on a schedule.

Speed to submit

The time from receiving a requisition to submitting qualified candidates. On high-volume staffing desks, faster speed to submit wins more reqs, which is why sourcing and outreach automation matter.
